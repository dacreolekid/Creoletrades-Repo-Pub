The Core Idea

Three zones, one map

Loup Apex+ stretches a Fibonacci ladder across the chart's whole history — from the all-time low to the held all-time high — and classifies where price sits in that range right now. Price is always in one of three zones:

🟦

Discount

The lower part of the all-time range — value territory, far from the highs.

🟧

Premium

The upper third, pressing toward the all-time high. Don't chase the extreme here.

🟩

Discovery

Beyond the held high — new all-time ground. This is where the extensions and the micro-fib take over.

Most fib tools only draw the retracement (0–1) zone. Loup Apex+ keeps building structure past 1.0, into price discovery — that's the whole point of the +.
The Engine

The Held All-Time Anchor

A fib is only as good as its anchor. Loup Apex+ uses an asymmetric anchor: the origin side is the true all-time extreme, and the trend side is a pullback-confirmed swing that holds.

Origin = true extreme

In an uptrend the 1.0 line sits on the genuine all-time low — the real floor of the entire move. (Mirrored to the all-time high in a downtrend.)

Trend side = held swing

The 0 line locks onto a swing the moment price pulls back pbATR×ATR from a new high, then holds there — so a fresh run trades inside the extension zone above it.

Why "held," not a live high? A normal all-time-high anchor can never be exceeded — so price could never sit in the extensions. And a symmetric pivot needs bars after a high to confirm, so on a parabola it strands at an ancient level. The pullback-confirmed swing stays recent and lets price discover above it. Tune it with Pullback to confirm anchor (×ATR) — lower hugs price tighter, higher only anchors on major swings.

The Standout

Fib-in-Fib Micro — even in discovery

Loup Apex+ finds the band price is currently sitting in — between any two adjacent levels — and subdivides it with a nested micro-fib. As price moves from band to band, the micro re-anchors automatically.

🎯

The active band

The two levels bracketing price are detected every bar. Turn on Highlight Active Band to shade it so "where am I + next level up/down" pops.

🚀

Micro in the extension zone

The breakthrough: when price is discovering new highs between, say, 1.272 and 1.618, you still get a full micro-fib inside that extension band. Structure where there used to be none.

Default micro levels: 0.236 · 0.382 · 0.5 · 0.618 · 0.786 of the active band. Each is editable, and Smart Labels hide any micro tag that would collide with a major level.
The Read

The All-Time Discovery Grade

A single color-coded label that tells you where price sits in its entire history and how hard it's breaking new ground. Built from three observed inputs — never a prediction.

DISCOUNT

Lower third of the all-time range. Value territory.

MID-RANGE

Middle of the range — no edge from position alone.

⚠ PREMIUM

Upper third (≥66%), pressing the all-time high. Caution chasing.

▲ DISCOVERY

Beyond the held high — price discovery is live, in new all-time ground.

🔥 DEEP DISCOVERY

Deep in the extensions (≥1.618) with strong conviction. The trend is running.

The three inputs behind the grade

InputWhat it measures
Position (% of range)0% = origin, 100% = the held extreme, >100% = discovery.
Extension depth (×range)How far beyond the held extreme price has traveled, in multiples of the all-time range.
ConvictionATRs gained in the trend direction over the lookback → STRONG (≥1.5) · STEADY (0.5–1.5) · FADING (<0.5).
Example label: ▲ DISCOVERY · 112% of range · +0.27× ext · STRONG. On deep-history names the all-time low can be near zero, so % of range reads high near the highs — that's expected; a pulled-back name will correctly read MID-RANGE / DISCOUNT.

Putting It Together

How to Use It

Read the Discovery Grade first

DISCOUNT = value, PREMIUM = near the high (don't chase), ▲ DISCOVERY / 🔥 DEEP DISCOVERY = price discovery is live and running.

Trade the micro-fib inside the active band

The highlighted band is where price is negotiating now. Use its micro 0.5 / 0.618 for entries and the band edges for invalidation — including the extension bands at new highs.

Use the extensions as targets

Once price breaks into discovery, 1.272 / 1.618 / 2.0 are your measured-move objectives. The Next-up distance in the debug tells you how far.

Let alerts watch for you

Set "Discovery entered" and the extension-target alerts so you don't have to babysit the chart.

Tip: drop the indicator on a higher timeframe (1D/1W) for the cleanest all-time read; the held anchor + Discovery Grade are most meaningful there.
