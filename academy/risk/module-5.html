The Wild West of Trading

Crypto is 24/7, unregulated in many jurisdictions, and 3-5x more volatile than equities. Bitcoin can move 10% in hours. Altcoins can move 30-50% in a day. The same principles apply — but the stakes are amplified.

You thought equity risk management was tough? Crypto is equity risk management with the volume cranked to eleven and no circuit breakers. There's no closing bell. There's no weekend off. Markets never sleep, and neither can your risk discipline.

"Crypto doesn't sleep. Your risk management can't either." — CreoleTrades Doctrine

Volatility Management

Crypto's average daily range dwarfs equities. Position sizing must be adjusted down. If you risk 1% on stocks, consider 0.5% on crypto. Wider stops are needed to avoid being stopped out by normal noise.

Think about it: a 5% intraday swing is a disaster in equities. In crypto, that's a quiet afternoon. You need to adjust your position sizing accordingly, or you'll be stopped out by the market's natural breathing.

Volatility

The magnitude and frequency of price swings. Crypto volatility is 3-5x higher than equities.

Average True Range (ATR)

The average of the true range over a period. Use this to set stops that account for normal market movement.

Liquidation Price

The price at which your leveraged position is forcefully closed by the exchange.

Funding Rate

In perpetual futures, traders pay each other. High funding rates indicate extreme leverage — a sign of danger.

Exchange & Custodial Risk

Your broker going bankrupt in stocks is rare and SIPC-insured. In crypto? Exchanges have collapsed: FTX, Celsius, and countless others. Billions lost. Users had no recourse.

This isn't theoretical. This is the new frontier of risk you have to manage.

How to Manage Exchange Risk

1 Assess the Exchange
Check: Is it regulated? Who owns it? What insurance exists? How long has it been operating? Does it have a history of hacks?
2 Split Your Funds
Never keep more than you need on an exchange. Active trading capital on the exchange. Long-term holds in cold storage. Emergency capital in a second exchange.
3 Understand Custody
Custodial (exchange holds your keys): Convenient, risky if exchange fails. Non-custodial (you hold your keys): Inconvenient, you're the only point of failure.

Leverage in Crypto

Many crypto exchanges offer 20x-125x leverage. At 100x leverage, a 1% move liquidates you. This isn't trading. This is gambling.

Let's be clear: leverage in crypto isn't a tool for smart traders. It's a tool for the house. They win your money whether you win or lose — they take the friction. Every liquidation that happens, the exchange keeps the fees. They're incentivized to watch you blow up.

Leverage Rules for Crypto

1 Maximum Leverage Rule
If you use leverage in crypto, keep it to 3x-5x maximum. Anything above that is gambling. You're not trading, you're hoping.
2 Liquidation Price Calculation
At 50x leverage: 1% move = liquidation. At 10x leverage: 10% move = liquidation. At 5x leverage: 20% move = liquidation. At 3x leverage: 33% move = liquidation.

At 3x, you can survive normal crypto volatility. Above that, you're gambling against the market's natural swings.
3 Position Size Adjustment
If you risk 0.5% on crypto (already reduced from equities), leverage doesn't change that. Your total account risk stays the same. Leverage just lets you control more with less capital — which means you're taking on more risk, not the same risk.

DeFi & Smart Contract Risk

Yield farming, staking, liquidity pools — all carry smart contract risk. Rug pulls, protocol exploits, impermanent loss. These are features of DeFi, not bugs.

Rule: only allocate money you can afford to lose entirely. Diversify across protocols. Check audits. And if you can't explain how a protocol makes money, you don't understand the risk. And if you don't understand the risk, you're the risk.

DeFi Risk Assessment

1 Understand the Yield Source
If a protocol offers 200% APY, where does it come from? Inflation? Liquidations? If the yield source is unclear or depends on unsustainable inflows, the protocol is eating itself.
2 Check the Audit
Has it been audited by a reputable firm? What did they find? Even audited protocols can fail, but unaudited ones are a lottery ticket.
3 Position Sizing Rule
Only allocate what you can afford to lose entirely. If you stake $10K in a new protocol offering 300% APY, you should be okay losing all $10K. If you're not, you don't belong in that protocol.

Key Takeaways

  • Crypto is 3-5x more volatile than equities — position sizing must be adjusted down (0.5% instead of 1%)
  • Exchange collapse is a real risk (FTX, Celsius) — never keep all your crypto on one exchange
  • Leverage above 3-5x is gambling, not trading — a 1% move at 100x leverage liquidates you
  • DeFi yields can't be free — if you can't explain how a protocol makes money, you're the product
  • Smart contract risk is permanent — only allocate capital you can afford to lose entirely
  • Crypto doesn't sleep, and neither should your risk discipline

Practice Exercise

Check your crypto portfolio right now. How much is sitting on exchanges vs. cold storage? Calculate what you'd lose if your primary exchange went down tomorrow. Then calculate your position sizes using the 0.5% rule instead of 1%. How does that change your approach?
