Options Trading Glossary

A complete reference of essential terms used in options trading. Keep this handy as you study and trade.

Core Concepts

Call Option

The right (but not obligation) to buy an underlying asset at a specified strike price on or before the expiration date. Calls gain value when the stock rises.

Put Option

The right (but not obligation) to sell an underlying asset at a specified strike price on or before the expiration date. Puts gain value when the stock falls.

Premium

The price paid to buy an option or received to sell one. Quoted per share; multiply by 100 to get the actual dollar amount.

Strike Price

The fixed price at which an option holder can exercise the option to buy (call) or sell (put) the underlying asset. Also called exercise price.

Expiration Date

The date on which an options contract expires and becomes worthless if not exercised. For stocks, this is typically the third Friday of each month.

In-The-Money (ITM)

An option with intrinsic value. For calls: stock price > strike. For puts: stock price < strike. ITM options have real, measurable value.

At-The-Money (ATM)

An option where the stock price equals (or is very close to) the strike price. ATM options have the most time value and highest gamma. Most liquid.

Out-The-Money (OTM)

An option with no intrinsic value. For calls: stock price < strike. For puts: stock price > strike. OTM options are cheaper but decay faster.

Values and Greeks

Intrinsic Value

The real, mathematical value of an option if exercised immediately. For calls: max(0, stock - strike). For puts: max(0, strike - stock).

Time Value

The portion of an option's premium that exists because of the time remaining until expiration. Time value decreases daily (theta decay).

Delta (Δ)

The rate at which an option's price changes for every $1 move in the underlying stock. Ranges from 0 to 1 for calls, -1 to 0 for puts. Approximates probability of expiring ITM.

Gamma (Γ)

The rate of change of delta. Measures how fast delta accelerates. Highest at ATM, especially near expiration. High gamma = high sensitivity to stock moves.

Theta (Θ)

Time decay. The rate at which an option loses value purely from time passing (all else equal). Negative for long options (cost), positive for short options (benefit).

Vega (ν)

Volatility sensitivity. The amount an option's price changes for every 1% change in implied volatility. Longer-dated options have higher vega.

Implied Volatility (IV)

The market's expectation of future volatility, implied by the option's price. Higher IV = higher option premiums. IV spikes signal uncertainty or strong moves expected.

Historical Volatility

Actual realized volatility of the underlying stock over a historical period (e.g., 30 days, 52 weeks). Used to compare against IV to identify mispricing.

Market Mechanics

Bid-Ask Spread

The difference between the bid price (what buyers offer) and ask price (what sellers demand). Tighter spreads indicate higher liquidity. Wider spreads increase transaction costs.

Volume

The number of contracts traded in a given period (typically per day). Higher volume indicates liquidity and active interest in that contract.

Open Interest

The total number of option contracts outstanding (not yet closed or exercised). Accumulated interest; shows historical significance of a contract.

Sweep Order

A large order executed across multiple exchanges simultaneously to avoid signaling. Indicates institutional activity and conviction. Bullish or bearish depending on whether calls or puts are being swept.

Block Trade

A large negotiated trade between institutions, executed off-exchange. Shows patience and systematic positioning. Often signals major institutional repositioning.

Unusual Options Activity (UOA)

Volume spikes, IV expansion, or unusual pricing that signals institutional positioning. Typically 5-10x normal volume in a specific strike. Used to confirm chart-based setups.

Strategies and Positions

Long Call

Buying a call option. Used when bullish. Max profit is unlimited, max loss is the premium paid. Theta (time decay) works against you.

Long Put

Buying a put option. Used when bearish. Max profit is (strike - premium paid), max loss is the premium paid. Theta works against you.

Bull Call Spread

Buy a call at one strike, sell a call at higher strike. Reduces cost (and theta impact). Limits max profit. Good for defined risk on smaller accounts.

Bear Put Spread

Sell a put at one strike, buy a put at lower strike. You collect net premium. Defines max loss. Requires capital/margin. Theta works for you.

Covered Call

Own 100 shares, sell a call against them. Generates income (theta works for you). Caps upside profit. Used for income on holdings.

Protective Put

Own stock, buy a put. Hedges downside risk. Like insurance. Costs premium (theta works against you). Used to protect valuable positions.

Advanced Terms

IV Rank

Implied volatility's rank over a 52-week period. Shows whether current IV is high or low relative to its year-long history. 0-100 scale.

IV Percentile

Similar to IV Rank but measures the percentage of days IV was below the current IV. Shows where current IV sits in historical distribution.

Volatility Crush

A sharp decline in implied volatility, usually after an event (earnings, FOMC, etc.). Hurts long option positions (vega loss) regardless of direction.

Exercise

The act of using your option right. For a call, you buy the stock at strike price. For a put, you sell the stock at strike price. Most retail traders close instead of exercising.

Assignment

When a short option is exercised against you. If you sold a call, you're assigned to sell 100 shares. If you sold a put, you're assigned to buy 100 shares at the strike.

Options Chain

A table showing all available option contracts for a given expiration. Displays strikes, bids, asks, volume, OI, IV, and Greeks for both calls and puts.
