STACK Academy Final Exam

20 questions covering all 6 modules. Click your answer — no second chances per question.

1. Smart Money engineers price movement through:

Technical indicator signals
Liquidity manipulation and accumulation strategies
Following retail trader sentiment
Random market fluctuations
Smart Money doesn't predict — they engineer moves by manipulating liquidity and accumulating positions strategically.

2. A Fair Value Gap is identified by:

A single large candle with high volume
Three candles where the wicks of candle 1 and candle 3 don't overlap
Any gap between the opening and closing price
Two consecutive candles moving in the same direction
An FVG is a three-candle imbalance where the wicks of the first and third candles don't overlap — leaving a gap that institutions fill.

3. During the Manipulation phase, Smart Money:

Quietly builds positions without moving price
Pushes price into stop-loss zones to trigger cascading stops and collect liquidity
Exits all positions and goes to cash
Follows the trend set by retail traders
Manipulation = liquidity sweep. Smart Money spikes price into retail stop zones, triggers stops, collects that liquidity, then reverses.

4. A Break of Structure (BOS) signals:

A trend reversal
A continuation of the current trend
A period of consolidation
An invalid setup
BOS = continuation. Price takes out a previous high/low in the direction of the existing trend, confirming momentum.

5. External structure refers to:

Structure on a different asset or market
The major swing highs and lows defining the overall trend
Structure below the 15-minute timeframe
Support and resistance from indicators
External = the macro swings. Internal = the smaller moves happening inside those swings.

6. In bullish structure, a CHoCH occurs when price breaks below:

A previous higher high
A previous higher low
The 50% range
Any support level
In a bullish trend (HH, HL), the CHoCH is when price breaks below a previous higher low — the first sign bulls are losing control.

7. Order block refinement involves:

Adding multiple indicators to confirm the OB
Dropping to a lower timeframe to narrow the zone for a tighter entry
Waiting for the OB to be tested three times
Only trading OBs on the daily timeframe
Refinement = lower timeframe drill-down to find the specific candle within the OB zone where institutional orders concentrated.

8. Smart Money buys at discount and sells at premium. The discount zone is:

Above 50% of the range
Below 50% of the range
At the exact midpoint of the range
Wherever volume is highest
Below 50% = discount (where institutions buy). Above 50% = premium (where they sell). This is core institutional risk management.

9. A liquidity sweep followed by displacement confirms:

Retail traders are in control
The market is random and unpredictable
Smart Money collected liquidity and is now pushing in their intended direction
The trend is about to reverse against the displacement
Sweep + displacement = Smart Money grabbed the liquidity they needed and now they're moving price with conviction.

10. The T in STACK stands for:

Timeframe
Target
Trigger — the specific price action event that activates entry
Trend
T = Trigger. Wait for the specific event — displacement, FVG fill, LTF CHoCH — before executing.

11. Which of the following is NOT a valid STACK trigger?

Displacement from a key level
FVG fill and rejection
A "gut feeling" that price will reverse
CHoCH on a lower timeframe at a key level
Gut feelings are predictions, not confirmations. The STACK Method only recognizes price-action-based triggers.

12. Anticipating a trigger before it fires means you are:

Trading with an edge
Predicting instead of confirming — violating core STACK doctrine
Being proactive and disciplined
Getting a better entry price
No trigger = no entry. Anticipation is prediction. Confirmation over prediction — always.

13. The STACK timeframe hierarchy from highest to lowest is:

Daily → 4H → 1H → 15min
Monthly → Weekly → Daily → 4H → 2H
Weekly → Daily → 4H → 2H → 1H
4H → 2H → 1H → 30min → 15min
Monthly → Weekly → Daily → 4H → 2H. Start with the macro and drill down to execution.

14. Full alignment means:

Your indicator gives a buy signal
At least two timeframes agree
All timeframes from Monthly through 2H point the same direction
The daily and 4H agree, regardless of other timeframes
Full alignment = ALL timeframes agree. That's the A+ Confirmation Stack.

15. When timeframes are misaligned, the STACK Method says:

Take the trade anyway with full size
Trade with reduced size/tighter stops, or wait for alignment
Only trade the highest timeframe direction
Switch to a different asset
Misalignment = lower conviction. Reduce size, tighten stops, or stand down entirely until alignment returns.

16. The Roux Score grades a setup by scoring:

Risk-to-reward ratio on a 1-5 scale
Each STACK letter (S, T, A, C, K) as 0 or 1, with 5/5 being A+
The number of confirming indicators
How many timeframes are bullish
S+T+A+C+K, each scored 0 or 1. 5/5 = A+ Confirmation Stack. The Roux Score is the final check before execution.

17. At PT1, a disciplined STACK trader should:

Close the entire position
Move stop loss to breakeven and add more size
Trim a portion of the position to lock in gains
Ignore it and hold for PT3
PT1 = first trim. Lock in partial gains, then manage the rest toward PT2 and PT3. Progressive profit-taking is disciplined execution.

18. When invalidation is hit, the correct action is:

Move the stop further and give it room
Average down to lower your cost basis
Exit immediately — the thesis is broken
Wait for the next candle to confirm
Invalidation = exit. No exceptions. No hoping. No moving stops. The K doesn't negotiate.

19. The C in STACK refers to:

Conviction — how confident you feel about the trade
Confirmation — Wolf Chart 2.1 confirms the setup is ready
Capital — how much money to risk
Consolidation — wait for price to range before entering
C = Confirmation via Wolf Chart 2.1. It's the technical confirmation system that validates the setup before execution.

20. The core CreoleTrades doctrine that applies to every letter of STACK is:

The trend is your friend
Buy the dip, sell the rip
Confirmation over prediction
Always trade with the crowd
Confirmation over prediction. Always. This is the thread that runs through every letter — Structure confirms bias, Trigger confirms entry, Alignment confirms conviction, Confirmation validates execution, Keep Discipline confirms risk management.
