DCA Final Exam

15 questions covering all 3 modules. Click your answer — no second chances per question. Score 12+ to pass.

1. What is the primary advantage of DCA over lump sum investing?

It guarantees higher returns.
It removes the emotional temptation to sell during downturns.
It eliminates all market risk.
It requires no discipline.
While lump sum wins mathematically 70% of the time, DCA wins in real life because it prevents panic-selling during market crashes.

2. In a 10-year DCA scenario with $500/month at 10% returns, approximately how much of the final value comes from compounding?

$20,000
$35,000
$60,000
$95,000
Total invested is $60K. Final value is ~$95K. Earnings from compounding = $35K. This shows why time is the ultimate wealth creator.

3. Which DCA frequency is recommended for most investors?

Weekly
Monthly
Quarterly
Annually
Monthly aligns with paychecks, reduces transaction fees, and is simple to remember. It's the professional standard.

4. What does Value Averaging do differently than DCA?

It invests more when prices are high.
It adjusts contribution amounts to reach a target portfolio value.
It eliminates the need for discipline.
It only works for bonds.
Value Averaging dynamically adjusts contributions based on market performance, making it more tactical and often slightly outperforming DCA.

5. What percentage of gross income should most professionals target for DCA?

1-2%
5-10%
15-20%
50%+
5-10% is the professional standard. It builds substantial wealth over time without crushing cash flow.

6. When prices drop 20% in DCA, what should you do?

Stop investing to avoid losses.
Continue regular DCA or increase contributions (tactical DCA).
Sell your existing holdings.
Wait for prices to recover.
Drops are buying opportunities in DCA. Your fixed amount buys more shares at the lower price, which increases profits during recovery.

7. What is Tactical DCA?

Selling when markets are down.
Increasing contributions during significant market dips.
Trading options exclusively.
Avoiding volatility at all costs.
Tactical DCA lets you maintain baseline DCA but add extra capital during 15-20% drops. This systematically buys fear.

8. What tax lot method is most tax-efficient for selling DCA shares?

FIFO
Specific Lot Identification
LIFO
Weighted Average
Specific Lot ID lets you sell your highest-cost shares first, minimizing capital gains. FIFO often triggers the most taxes.

9. How should you rebalance your DCA portfolio tax-efficiently?

Sell winners, buy losers monthly.
Direct new contributions to underweight positions.
Sell everything and reinvest annually.
Never rebalance—it triggers taxes.
Direct new contributions to underweight assets. This rebalances without selling, avoiding capital gains taxes entirely.

10. What's the main advantage of using your 401(k) for DCA?

It guarantees returns.
Automatic payroll deduction, tax benefits, potential employer match (free money).
It's the only way to invest.
It eliminates market risk.
401(k) is DCA on steroids—automatic deduction, pre-tax contributions, and employer match if available. Maximize this first.

11. What happens to your cost basis when prices drop during DCA?

It increases.
It decreases because you buy more shares at the lower price.
It stays the same.
It becomes irrelevant.
Your cost basis naturally lowers over time as you buy more at lower prices. This is the mathematical foundation of DCA's power.

12. Which asset type makes DCA most critical due to extreme volatility?

Index funds
Bonds
Cryptocurrency
Dividend stocks
Crypto's extreme volatility makes timing impossible. DCA is essential because you can't predict Bitcoin's direction.

13. What's the difference between starting DCA at 25 vs 35 with $500/month at 10% returns by age 65?

$100,000
$500,000+
$250,000
$50,000
Starting at 25 yields ~$1.2M. Starting at 35 yields ~$520K. The 10-year gap = $680K+ in lost compounding.

14. What advantage do cash-secured puts offer to DCA investors?

They guarantee profits.
You collect premium income and potentially buy at a discount if assigned.
They eliminate volatility.
They work for all investors.
Cash-secured puts let you earn premium while waiting. If assigned, you buy at a discount. Over time, this reduces your cost basis below pure DCA.

15. Why does DCA work psychologically where most investors fail?

It removes all risk.
It removes the emotional need to time the market and prevents panic-selling.
It guarantees market beating returns.
It makes trading decisions easier.
DCA's power is psychological—you eliminate the choice to sell at the worst time. Discipline beats timing every time.
