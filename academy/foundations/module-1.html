Reading the Language of Price

Every candle tells a story. Before indicators, before patterns, before anything — you need to read candles. This is the alphabet of trading.

Think about it: you wouldn't try to read a book in a language you don't understand. The same applies here. Candles are your letters. The patterns they form are words. The trends they create are sentences. Master the letters first, and everything else becomes readable.

CreoleTrades voice: "You can't read a book if you don't know the letters. Candles are your letters."

This module is about building that foundation. Not flashy. Not complicated. Just fundamental skills that every professional trader uses every single day.

Anatomy of a Candlestick

A candlestick is simple. It shows four pieces of information for a time period (5-minute candle, hourly, daily, whatever):

  • Open: The opening price of the period
  • Close: The closing price of the period
  • High: The highest price reached during the period
  • Low: The lowest price reached during the period

The Body vs. The Wick

The candle has two parts:

  • Body (or Real Body): The rectangular block between open and close. The size tells you conviction.
  • Wick (or Shadow): The thin lines extending above and below the body. These show rejection — price went there but buyers/sellers rejected it.

Color Rules

  • Green/Bullish Candle: Close is above the open. Buyers won the period.
  • Red/Bearish Candle: Close is below the open. Sellers won the period.
  • Big Body: Conviction. One side dominated completely.
  • Small Body: Indecision. Neither side could control the period.
  • Long Wicks: Rejection. Price tested a level but was pushed back.

Vocabulary Grid

Body
The rectangular section of a candle representing the distance between open and close price.
Wick/Shadow
The thin lines extending from the body showing the high and low prices reached during the period.
Open
The price at which the candle period begins.
Close
The price at which the candle period ends.
High
The highest price reached during the candle period.
Low
The lowest price reached during the candle period.
Bullish Candle
A green candle where the close is higher than the open, indicating buying pressure.
Bearish Candle
A red candle where the close is lower than the open, indicating selling pressure.

Single Candle Patterns

These are the patterns that show up on ONE candle. Master these before moving to multi-candle patterns.

Doji

Pattern
Doji
Anatomy
Open equals close. The body is nearly non-existent.
Signal
Indecision. Neither buyers nor sellers won the period.

Hammer

Pattern
Hammer
Anatomy
Small body at the top, long lower wick. Looks like a hammer striking down.
Signal
Bullish reversal. Sellers pushed price down, but buyers rejected the lower price and pushed back up.

Shooting Star

Pattern
Shooting Star
Anatomy
Small body at the bottom, long upper wick. Opposite of hammer.
Signal
Bearish reversal. Buyers pushed price up, but sellers rejected the higher price and pushed back down.

Marubozu

Pattern
Marubozu
Anatomy
Full body, no wicks. Open and low are the same, or close and high are the same.
Signal
Pure momentum. One side completely dominated with no rejection at either end.

Spinning Top

Pattern
Spinning Top
Anatomy
Small body in the middle with equal-length wicks above and below.
Signal
Indecision. Neither side could gain control; price was pushed back from both directions equally.

Multi-Candle Patterns

Now we combine candles to see bigger stories. These patterns span 2-3+ candles.

Bullish Engulfing

How to Identify

1 Find the Setup
Look for a red (bearish) candle. This is your first candle.
2 Look for the Engulfment
The next candle is green (bullish) and its body completely encompasses (engulfs) the previous red candle's body. The green candle's low is lower than or equal to the red candle's low, and its high is higher than the red candle's high.
3 Confirm the Signal
This is a bullish reversal signal. Sellers controlled, but buyers came in with conviction and completely flipped the script.

Bearish Engulfing

How to Identify

1 Find the Setup
Look for a green (bullish) candle.
2 Look for the Engulfment
The next candle is red (bearish) and completely engulfs the previous green candle's body.
3 Confirm the Signal
Bearish reversal. Buyers were in control, but sellers entered with power and flipped momentum.

Morning Star

How to Identify

1 First Candle
A red (bearish) candle, showing weakness.
2 Second Candle
A small-bodied candle (any color), typically gapping lower or just closing with a small range. This shows exhaustion — selling pressure is running out.
3 Third Candle
A green (bullish) candle that closes above the midpoint of the first red candle. Buyers take control and reverse the downtrend.

Evening Star

How to Identify

1 First Candle
A green (bullish) candle, showing strength.
2 Second Candle
A small-bodied candle (any color), typically gapping higher or just closing with a small range. Shows exhaustion in the uptrend.
3 Third Candle
A red (bearish) candle that closes below the midpoint of the first green candle. Sellers take control and reverse the uptrend.

Context Is Everything

This is critical: a hammer at the bottom of a downtrend = bullish reversal signal. A hammer in the middle of a sideways range = nothing. A hammer in the middle of an uptrend = irrelevant noise.

The pattern itself matters less than where it appears. This is the principle that separates professionals from amateurs.

The rule: Always ask WHERE on the chart the candle appeared, not just WHAT the candle is.

Is it at the bottom of a downtrend? (Bullish context)

Is it at the top of an uptrend? (Bearish context)

Is it in the middle of nowhere? (Neutral or irrelevant)

This principle sets up everything you'll learn later about Support & Resistance, and Smart Money Concepts. The chart location matters more than the pattern shape.

Key Takeaways

  1. Candlesticks show four prices: Open, Close, High, Low. The body is the story; the wick is the rejection.
  2. A big body = conviction. A small body = indecision. Long wicks = rejection of price levels.
  3. Green candles = close above open (bullish). Red candles = close below open (bearish).
  4. Single candle patterns (Doji, Hammer, Shooting Star, Marubozu, Spinning Top) signal indecision, momentum, or reversal on their own.
  5. Multi-candle patterns (Engulfing, Morning Star, Evening Star) tell stories across multiple periods and show stronger reversal signals.
  6. Context trumps pattern. A hammer at the bottom of a downtrend is powerful. A hammer in a sideways range is noise. Always ask WHERE, not just WHAT.
