Support & Resistance

The foundation of every technical setup

The Foundation of Every Setup

Support and resistance are the most important concepts in all of technical analysis. Every strategy, every pattern, every indicator — they all reference levels where price has reacted before. If you only learn ONE thing from this course, learn to read S/R levels.

This module gives you the tools to identify, draw, and use support and resistance levels that will transform how you read charts. These are the invisible guardrails that professional traders watch constantly.

What Is Support?

A price level where buying pressure has historically overcome selling pressure. Price bounces here. Think of it as a floor — price falls to it and gets pushed back up.

Key insight: The more times price bounces off support, the stronger it is. BUT: every support level eventually breaks. When it does, be ready.

What Is Resistance?

A price level where selling pressure has historically overcome buying pressure. Price rejects here. Think of it as a ceiling — price rises to it and gets pushed back down.

Critical concept: When resistance breaks, it often becomes support (flip). This is one of the most reliable patterns in trading.

Support/Resistance Flips

This is huge for beginners. When price breaks above resistance, that level becomes support. When price breaks below support, that level becomes resistance.

Why does this happen?

The traders who sold at resistance are now underwater and want to get out at breakeven — their selling becomes new resistance (or their buying becomes new support when the flip reverses).

The S/R Flip Breakdown

Step 1: Resistance forms — Price rallies to $100 and gets rejected (sellers take profit). This becomes resistance.

Step 2: Breakout happens — Price breaks above $100 on heavy volume. Those sellers are now trapped short.

Step 3: The flip — Price pulls back to $100. Those trapped sellers rush to cover (buy) at breakeven. $100 is now support instead of resistance.

Psychological Levels

Round numbers matter: $100, $200, $400, $500. SPY at $500 is a bigger deal than SPY at $503.

Why round numbers are invisible S/R levels:

  • Options strikes cluster at round numbers. Huge options activity = price reaction.
  • Algorithms are programmed around them. Major institutions execute orders at round numbers.
  • Retail traders watch them. When everyone expects a reaction, it becomes self-fulfilling.
  • News and media highlight round-number milestones. "SPY hits $500!" drives attention and liquidity.

Action item: Always mark round numbers on your chart as potential S/R levels, even if price hasn't bounced there yet.

Volume at Price (VRVP)

Volume doesn't just happen over time — it happens at specific prices. The Volume at Range Visible Profile (VRVP) shows you WHERE the most trading happened.

How to read VRVP:

  • High-volume nodes = strong S/R. Many traders bought/sold there. Price will interact with it again.
  • Low-volume nodes = price moves through quickly. Nobody fought for that price level — it's likely just a runner-through.
  • Volume clusters at S/R levels confirm that those levels matter. If high volume happens at resistance, that resistance is likely to hold (or break with authority).

This is an advanced concept but critical: Understanding WHY certain levels hold is the difference between drawing random lines and identifying real S/R that institutions respect.

Key Takeaways

  • Support = floor where price bounces. Resistance = ceiling where price rejects.
  • The more times price tests a level and holds, the stronger that level is.
  • When resistance breaks, it becomes support (the S/R flip). This creates high-probability setup zones.
  • Round numbers like $100, $500, $200 act as psychological S/R levels even without prior price history.
  • Volume at Price reveals WHERE traders actually fought for price — high-volume nodes are strong S/R.
  • Every support level eventually breaks. Be ready to treat it as new resistance when it does.

Practice Exercise

On SPY daily chart, do this now:

  1. Mark the 3 most obvious support levels from the last 6 months.
  2. Mark the 3 most obvious resistance levels from the last 6 months.
  3. Check: did any of them flip (support became resistance or vice versa)?
  4. Are any of them near psychological round numbers?
  5. Add VRVP to your chart — do the high-volume nodes line up with your manually drawn levels?

This exercise locks the concepts into your brain. Don't skip it.

Test Your Knowledge

Question 1 What makes a support level "strong"?
Correct Answer:
Multiple bounces off the same level — the more times price tests support and holds, the stronger it is. A level tested once is weak. A level tested 5+ times is a fortress.
Question 2 When resistance breaks, what does it typically become?
Correct Answer:
Support — this is called an S/R flip, and it's one of the most reliable patterns in trading. Trapped sellers at the breakout level will rush to cover (buy) when price pulls back, creating new support.
Question 3 Why do round numbers like $100 or $500 act as support/resistance?
Correct Answer:
Psychological levels attract attention from traders, options activity clusters there, and algorithms are programmed to execute at round numbers. This creates a self-fulfilling prophecy where round numbers become real S/R levels.
Question 4 What does a high-volume node on VRVP indicate?
Correct Answer:
A price level where heavy trading occurred — these tend to act as strong support or resistance because many traders have positions there. High-volume nodes = places where price historically fights.
Question 5 Price breaks below a support level. What should you expect that level to become?
Correct Answer:
Resistance — trapped buyers who held through the break will sell when price returns to that level to minimize losses. This is the inverse of the S/R flip pattern.
