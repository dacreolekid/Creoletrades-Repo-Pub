The Ceasefire Rally Looked Like a Gift. The Oil Whipsaw Was the Receipt.

The US–Iran ceasefire sent the Dow up 1,325 points and oil crashing 16% in a single session. Then oil ripped back above $100 the next day. This week’s Roux Journal breaks down why the C in STACK would have kept you out of the whipsaw — and why bank earnings next week demand the same discipline.

✍ Coach Que 📅 April 12, 2026 ⏱ ~6 min read 🏷 STACK Method · Confirmation · Oil · Iran · Bank Earnings

Tuesday morning felt like Christmas.

The US and Iran agreed to a two-week ceasefire barely an hour before Trump’s deadline to, in his words, obliterate the country. Pakistan brokered the deal. The Strait of Hormuz was supposed to reopen. And every screen on Wall Street lit up green.

The Dow jumped 1,325 points — its best day in over a year. The S&P closed up 2.5%. The Nasdaq gained 2.8%. Oil cratered 16.4% to $94.41 a barrel, the largest single-day drop since April 2020. Energy names that had been running for weeks suddenly reversed. Defense stocks pulled back. The whole market exhaled.

If you were watching the tape Tuesday morning, every instinct said the same thing: the war trade is over. Get in on the relief rally. Buy the dip in tech. Short oil. The structure looked clean. The trigger fired. The move was obvious.

And then Wednesday happened.

The Whipsaw Nobody Planned For

Oil ripped back above $100 by Thursday morning. Why? Because the Strait of Hormuz never actually reopened. Iran limited vessel traffic despite the ceasefire. Maritime intelligence confirmed the strait was in what they called a “supervised pause” — Iranian armed forces still controlled all transits. The diplomatic calendar said peace. The shipping lanes said otherwise.

The traders who shorted oil Tuesday afternoon and woke up Thursday to a 6% reversal learned something expensive: the headline was not the confirmation. The ceasefire announcement was a trigger at best. For the setup to be real — for the relief trade to hold — you needed the Strait to physically reopen, tanker traffic to normalize, and the market to price in sustained supply recovery. None of that happened.

Structure said oil was extended. The trigger fired on the ceasefire headline. But the confirmation never came. The Strait stayed closed. The regime didn’t flip. The C didn’t fire.

And the traders who skipped the C paid for it.

Bank Earnings Are the Next Test

This week’s ceasefire whipsaw is a warm-up. The real volatility starts Monday.

Goldman Sachs reports Q1 earnings before the bell on April 13. JPMorgan follows Tuesday, April 14. Citigroup and Bank of America are in the same window. This is the first full earnings season since the Iran conflict began reshaping capital flows, and the numbers are going to tell a story that doesn’t fit neatly into a single narrative.

Goldman is expected to report a 16% EPS increase year-over-year, driven by a 26% surge in advisory and underwriting fees. Their investment banking backlog is at a four-year high. Over 120 global IPO filings hit in early 2026 — the most active start since the record-breaking 2021 cycle. The fee machine is running.

JPMorgan is projected to post a 7% rise in Q1 EPS to around $5.44, with an 18% increase in investment banking fees. Corporations are rushing to refinance what analysts are calling the “debt tsunami” needed to fund AI infrastructure expansion. JPM is eating that flow.

But here’s the catch: the federal funds rate has settled into a 3.50%–3.75% neutral corridor. The spread income that powered bank profits for the last two years has plateaued. If investment banking fees disappoint — or if management guides cautiously because of ceasefire uncertainty — the reaction could be violent. These stocks have been priced for a clean transition from rate income to fee income. If that transition stutters, the sell-off won’t wait for your analysis.

Earnings Week Cheat Sheet
  • Goldman Sachs (GS) — Monday, April 13 pre-market. Watch advisory fees and IPO backlog guidance.
  • JPMorgan Chase (JPM) — Tuesday, April 14 pre-market. Watch IB fee growth and NII guidance.
  • The setup is there. The trigger fires on earnings. But the C — the confirmation — won’t be known until you read the report and watch the first hour of price action.

If you enter before the C fires, you’re trading a prediction. Not a confirmation.

What This Week Taught Us About the C

The STACK rotation landed on Confirmation this week, and the market handed us a live case study.

Tuesday’s ceasefire rally had everything a trader could want: a clear catalyst, massive institutional flow, and a directional move across every major index. Structure was bullish. The trigger was obvious. Alignment across equities was unanimous. Three letters passed.

But the C didn’t. Not on the oil trade. Not on the energy reversal plays. Not on the “war is over” thesis. Because confirmation isn’t about what the headline says. It’s about what the chart says after the headline fades.

Wolf Chart 2.1 reads the regime — not the news. It doesn’t know about ceasefires or diplomatic timelines or Strait of Hormuz logistics. It reads price, momentum, and regime state. And when the regime doesn’t flip, the gate doesn’t lock. And when the gate doesn’t lock, you don’t enter.

The traders who respected that — who saw the rally, acknowledged the trigger, checked the C, and waited — avoided the Thursday whipsaw entirely. They didn’t catch the first 3% of the move. But they also didn’t give back 6% two days later trying to hold a position that was never confirmed.

Next Week’s Playbook

Bank earnings will dominate Monday through Wednesday. Goldman and JPMorgan will set the tone. If the numbers confirm the fee-income transition and management guides with confidence despite the geopolitical fog, the rally has legs. If they don’t, the same traders who chased Tuesday’s ceasefire move will chase the earnings gap — and get caught the same way.

The playbook is the same either way: let the C fire. Don’t predict the earnings reaction. Don’t front-run the guidance. Wait for Wolf Chart to lock the regime on the 5-minute after the report drops. If it confirms, you execute with a full STACK behind you. If it doesn’t, you wait — even if every headline says go.

The ceasefire taught us this week. Bank earnings will test it next week. The lesson doesn’t change.

You don’t rush a roux. You don’t rush a trade.

Confirmation over prediction. Stay STACKED.
