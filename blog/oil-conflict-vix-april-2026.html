Oil, Conflict, and the VIX: What Traders Need to Watch This Month

Crude oil just broke $110 on conflict — not earnings. The VIX sits at 24. Here’s what the STACK Method says about trading headline-driven volatility without losing your process.

✍ Coach Que 📅 April 3, 2026 ⏱ ~4 min read 🏷 Oil · VIX · Geopolitics · STACK Method

The Setup

Crude oil just broke $110.

Not on earnings. Not on inventory data. On conflict. The Strait of Hormuz — the 21-mile chokepoint that moves roughly 20% of the world’s daily oil supply — has been functionally restricted since late February. And analysts are responding the only way they know how: by raising price targets.

WTI surged 11% over the past week alone. Brent crude is hovering near $109–$111. Analyst projections for April range from the mid-$80s to above $130, depending on how the geopolitical situation evolves. That’s not a forecast — that’s a confession that nobody knows what happens next.

The VIX Tells the Story

While oil rips higher, the VIX is doing something interesting. It spiked above 30 in March when the situation was at its most uncertain — traders were building panic positions, hedging everything, and pricing in worst-case scenarios across the board.

Now it’s pulled back to around 24. Still elevated. Still above the 20 line that separates “normal” from “pay attention.” But the retreat from 30 tells you something: the market is starting to digest the risk instead of reacting to it.

That doesn’t mean the risk is gone. It means institutions have priced in the current state of the conflict. If it escalates further — if Hormuz fully closes or a direct military confrontation widens — the VIX reprices again. Fast.

For traders, this is the environment where discipline matters most. The VIX at 24 means options premiums are inflated, ATR ranges are wider than normal, and false moves are more frequent. Every one of those conditions punishes traders who skip the process.

What the Analysts Are Saying

The range of predictions tells you everything. J.P. Morgan had Brent averaging around $60 for the year based on fundamentals. The EIA sees it staying above $95 through May before pulling back later in 2026. RaboBank warned that a prolonged conflict could produce an energy shock comparable to the 1970s.

When the spread between the lowest and highest credible forecast is $70+, you’re not trading a market — you’re trading a headline cycle. And headline cycles reward one thing: confirmation over prediction.

How This Connects to the STACK

This is a textbook T problem.

The T in STACK is Trigger — waiting for a specific, defined price action event before committing capital. In an environment like this, the trigger isn’t “oil is going up” or “the VIX is elevated.” Those are observations, not signals.

The trigger is structural. It’s a timeframe close above a key level. It’s a Wolf Chart state confirmation. It’s an Institutional Gate flipping from BEAR to BULL — or confirming the BEAR — on a timeframe that carries weight.

What it is not: a reaction to the next headline. The moment you trade the news instead of the chart, you’re not using the STACK. You’re gambling with a narrative attached.

Structure first. Always.

The K Doesn’t Negotiate

Elevated VIX environments are where the K in STACK earns its place. Position sizing gets tighter. Invalidation levels get respected, not moved. Wider ATR means your stops need room — and your ego needs to accept smaller position sizes to give them that room.

The traders who blow up in volatile markets aren’t the ones who get the direction wrong. They’re the ones who sized too big, moved their stop, or entered before the trigger fired because “it looked ready.”

It wasn’t ready. The T hadn’t fired. And the K — Keep Discipline — existed for exactly this moment.

What to Watch

Oil above $110 with Hormuz restricted is the macro backdrop. The VIX at 24 is the volatility tax. Together, they create an environment where every shortcut gets punished.

Trading This Environment
  • Every entry needs full STACK confirmation — no shortcuts.
  • Options DTE needs to account for inflated premium — don’t buy weeklies into elevated VIX.
  • Position sizing should reflect the wider ranges — the ATR is telling you the market moves bigger right now, so your risk per trade adjusts accordingly.
  • False moves are more frequent — that pending liquidity sweep you see below the obvious level? It’s coming. Wait for the sweep to complete before you enter.

The Bottom Line

Analysts can raise their price targets. Headlines can run. Oil can spike or crash on the next diplomatic development. None of that changes your process.

Read structure. Wait for the trigger. Confirm alignment. Let Wolf Chart confirm. And when it all lines up — execute with discipline.

If it doesn’t line up? Stand down. There’s always another setup.

You don’t rush a roux. You don’t rush a trade.

Confirmation over prediction. Stay STACKED.
